# Plan: Coefficient GCRCB Application + Project Onboarding Process

## Context

Beacon wants to apply to Coefficient Giving (formerly Open Philanthropy) GCRCB for an **organizational grant** ($400-500K, 12 months) covering Beacon's operating costs. This is separate from the project-level grants that sponsored projects (like Lens Academy) will apply for through SFF etc. Getting Coefficient funding would:
- Provide ~1 year of operating runway without stress (current AISTOF $250K seed runs out ~May at $50K/month burn)
- Allow upscaling if demand rises — EOR deposits, additional project infrastructure
- Bridge to self-sustainability from admin fees + AUM interest
- The existing AISTOF grant from JueYan is a signal of confidence for other evaluators

Simultaneously, this accelerates two connected workstreams:
1. **Model A fiscal sponsorship agreement** — coming back from Paul (attorney), needs Lisha to solidify the onboarding process
2. **Project proposal template** — the Confluence template at PM/pages/67371009 needs fleshing out to become the intake form for incoming projects

A 3-month decision timeline (Coefficient's standard) would mean decision by ~July, funds by ~August — that's tight against the cash flow projections showing negative balance without topup. Worth stating honestly.

### Project Proposal Template (Confluence)
- Current state: `PM/pages/67371009` — skeleton with basic fields
- Current fields: project name, grant status, grant amount, grantmaker, people details, 3-4 sentence description, longer proposals link, GCR relevance, failure modes assessment
- **Needs:** Flesh out into a proper intake form. Should align with what Beacon needs to evaluate projects AND what funders (like Coefficient) will ask about Beacon's project selection process.

  - Open science compliance (per Beacon's principles)
  - Staffing plan (who's doing the work, EOR needs)
  - Legal/compliance considerations (international operations, information hazards, etc.)
  - Requested sponsorship services (what do they need from Beacon beyond the tax wrapper?)
