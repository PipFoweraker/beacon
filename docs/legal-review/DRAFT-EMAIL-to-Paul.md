---

Hi Paul,

Thanks for the bylaws and three policies. I've reviewed them and they look solid. A few items below, roughly in order of importance.

**1. Fiscal year — please confirm alignment**

I want to confirm that the fiscal year is January 1 - December 31 (calendar year). The bylaws leave this to a Board resolution (Section 8.7), which is fine, but I want to make sure this is consistent with what we filed in the Certificate of Incorporation and with the Form 1023 application. If we stated a fiscal year in the incorporation docs, let's make sure the Board resolution matches. If not, we'll resolve this at the organizational meeting.

**2. Additional policy: Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption**

I've attached a draft Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy for Beacon. This is adapted from one I wrote for Ashgro (a similar GCR fiscal sponsor). I believe we need this to satisfy governance expectations from major funders in the space, particularly Open Philanthropy / Coefficient Giving's GCRCB program, which requires grantee organizations to meet minimum standards for organizational policies.

Could you please review the attached draft and either:
- Clean it up and make it consistent with the style and legal conventions of the other three policies, or
- Let me know if you'd prefer to redraft from scratch with the Ashgro version and my draft as reference.

The key substantive points I want preserved:
- Zero-tolerance statement
- FCPA reference (and other applicable legislation)
- Explicit prohibition of facilitation payments
- Positive obligation to report
- Coverage of fiscally sponsored project personnel
- Cross-reference to the Whistleblower and COI policies

**3. Minor changes to existing documents**

*Whistleblower Policy:*
- Typo: "the the full board" appears in the Reporting Procedure section (should be "the full board")

*Bylaws — items I'd like your view on:*
- Section 3.6 requires the annual meeting "in the first quarter of each fiscal year." Is there any risk to us if we miss this deadline in a given year (e.g., Q1 of the first year if we incorporate late in a quarter)? Would "within the first 120 days" or similar give us useful flexibility?
- Section 3.11 (written consent without meeting) requires ALL directors to consent. This is standard DGCL, I know, but practically it means one unresponsive director blocks all written consents. Is there a way to provide for a supermajority written consent, or is that not available under Delaware nonprofit law?

*Conflict of Interest Policy:*
- "Identified contractors" — could we add a sentence clarifying that the President (or Board) designates which contractors are covered persons for purposes of this policy? Currently it's ambiguous who does the identifying.

**4. Policies we still need (not urgent for Form 1023, but needed before we start accepting GCRCB-adjacent grants)**

Based on my research into Open Phil / Coefficient Giving's minimum standards for organizational policies, we will also need:
- Anti-harassment / sexual misconduct prevention policy (with complaint procedures and reporting)
- Code of conduct (possibly combined board + organizational)
- Anti-money laundering policy (for international grant disbursements and OFAC screening)

I've drafted both a Code of Conduct and an Anti-Harassment and Sexual Misconduct Prevention Policy myself — attached alongside the anti-bribery draft. The anti-harassment policy is designed to meet or exceed requirements across the US, UK, and Australia (our main operating jurisdictions), including the UK's incoming "all reasonable steps" standard (Employment Rights Act 2025, effective October 2026) and Australia's positive duty under the Respect@Work amendments.

I've flagged two specific questions for you inside the anti-harassment policy text with "[Attorney review note]" markers:

2. Whether the appeals process structure is appropriate

Please review both for legal soundness and consistency with the other governance documents. The code of conduct is straightforward; the anti-harassment policy is the one that needs real scrutiny.

The AML question I'll handle separately — likely need specialized advice given our international disbursement model.
