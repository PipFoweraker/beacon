**ANTI-BRIBERY AND ANTI-CORRUPTION POLICY**

**Introduction**

This policy outlines the commitment of Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk ("Beacon") to prevent bribery and corruption within its operations, including the projects it provides fiscal sponsorship to. The global catastrophic risk research field relies on high levels of integrity and transparency in order to shield against compliance, legal, and reputational risk. Beacon must operate according to best practice to mitigate these risks, and to satisfy the governance expectations of major funders in the catastrophic risk space.

**Scope**

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, and agents of Beacon. It also applies to any third parties acting on Beacon's behalf or representing its interests, including Project Directors and personnel of fiscally sponsored projects.

**Definition of Bribery and Corruption**

Precise definitions of bribery and corruption vary by country and legislative or regulatory body. For the purposes of this policy, Beacon takes an expansive definition, as follows:

*Bribery*: The offering, giving, receiving, or soliciting of any item of value to influence the actions of an official or other person in charge of a public or legal duty.

*Corruption*: The abuse of entrusted power for private gain, including activities such as fraud, embezzlement, and nepotism.

As Beacon is organized and operated exclusively for purposes described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, there are restrictions on its activity that prohibit certain actions that would result in private benefit. This policy does not specifically refer to those restrictions, but operates in parallel in order to ensure Beacon remains compliant with all applicable governance legislation, including its Conflict of Interest Policy.

**Governing Legislation and Standards**

Beacon is committed to complying with the following laws and standards as they apply to its operations and sponsored projects:

- U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- U.S. Travel Act (18 U.S.C. sections 1951-1952)
- State commercial bribery statutes applicable to Beacon's operations
- OECD Anti-Bribery Convention (to the extent applicable to international grant-making and contractor relationships)
- OFAC sanctions and screening requirements (as applicable to disbursements to international persons and entities)

Where Beacon's sponsored projects operate in jurisdictions with local anti-bribery or anti-corruption legislation (e.g., UK Bribery Act 2010, EU Anti-Fraud frameworks), Beacon expects those projects to comply with applicable local law and will incorporate compliance expectations into fiscal sponsorship agreements.

**Policy Statement**

Beacon has a zero-tolerance approach to bribery and corruption. Beacon is committed to acting professionally, fairly, and with integrity in all its operations and relationships, wherever it operates. Specifically:

1. No director, officer, employee, contractor, volunteer, agent, or sponsored project personnel shall offer, give, solicit, or accept any bribe, kickback, or corrupt payment of any kind.

2. Facilitation payments are prohibited. No payment shall be made to expedite or secure the performance of a routine governmental action.

3. Gifts and hospitality must comply with the limits established in Beacon's Conflict of Interest Policy (currently $50 per year per source). Any gift or hospitality that could reasonably be perceived as intended to influence a business or official decision must be declined and reported.

4. Charitable donations and sponsorships made by or through Beacon must not be used as a mechanism to conceal bribery or to circumvent this policy.

5. Beacon will conduct appropriate due diligence on third parties, contractors, and sponsored projects to assess bribery and corruption risk, proportionate to the nature and scale of the relationship.

**Reporting and Whistleblowing**

All covered persons are obliged to report any suspicion of bribery or corruption to their supervisor or through the channels designated in Beacon's Whistleblower Policy. There is a positive obligation to report; failure to do so may result in disciplinary action.

Reports will be investigated promptly and thoroughly, with appropriate actions taken against violators of this policy. The protections established in Beacon's Whistleblower Policy apply to any reports made in good faith under this policy.

**Record-Keeping**

Beacon will maintain accurate books and records that fairly reflect all transactions and dispositions of assets. No undisclosed or unrecorded accounts or funds shall be established or maintained for any purpose. All transactions must be properly and accurately recorded in Beacon's accounting system in accordance with Beacon's Document Retention Policy.

**Training**

Beacon will provide anti-bribery and anti-corruption awareness information to all directors, officers, and employees upon onboarding. As Beacon's operations grow, the Board or its designee will determine whether periodic training is warranted based on the nature and geographic scope of Beacon's activities.

**Monitoring and Review**

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Board to ensure its effectiveness. Any changes in legislation, funder requirements, or operational practices will be incorporated into the policy as necessary.

**Sanctions for Non-Compliance**

Non-compliance with this policy will result in disciplinary action, which may include termination of employment or contract, termination of a fiscal sponsorship arrangement, and legal action where appropriate.
