**CODE OF CONDUCT**

**Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk**

**Purpose**

This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior expected of all persons associated with Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk ("Beacon"). It reflects Beacon's commitment to operating with integrity, professionalism, and respect in pursuit of its charitable mission.

**Scope**

This Code applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, and agents of Beacon, as well as Project Directors and personnel of fiscally sponsored projects in their capacity as representatives of or participants in Beacon-affiliated activities. It applies to all work-related contexts, including offices, events, travel, online communications, and any setting where a person is acting on behalf of or representing Beacon.

**Standards of Conduct**

All covered persons shall:

1. **Act with integrity.** Be honest, transparent, and ethical in all dealings on behalf of Beacon. Do not misrepresent Beacon's positions, capabilities, or status.

2. **Respect others.** Treat all colleagues, project partners, grantors, grantees, and members of the public with dignity and respect. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, and intimidation are prohibited. (Beacon's Anti-Harassment and Sexual Misconduct Prevention Policy provides detailed definitions and procedures.)

3. **Avoid conflicts of interest.** Disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest in accordance with Beacon's Conflict of Interest Policy. Do not use your position for personal gain or the gain of family members or close associates.

4. **Protect Beacon's resources.** Use Beacon's funds, property, information, and other resources responsibly and only for authorized purposes. Do not use Beacon resources for personal benefit.

5. **Maintain confidentiality.** Protect confidential information about Beacon's operations, donors, sponsored projects, and personnel. Do not disclose confidential information except as authorized or legally required.

6. **Comply with law and policy.** Comply with all applicable laws and regulations, and with Beacon's adopted policies, including:
   - Conflict of Interest Policy
   - Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy
   - Anti-Harassment and Sexual Misconduct Prevention Policy
   - Whistleblower Policy
   - Document Retention Policy

7. **Report concerns.** Report suspected violations of this Code, of Beacon's policies, or of applicable law through the channels described in Beacon's Whistleblower Policy. Do not retaliate against anyone who makes a good-faith report.

8. **Represent Beacon responsibly.** When speaking or acting publicly in a way that could reasonably be associated with Beacon, be mindful that your conduct may reflect on the organization. Distinguish personal views from Beacon's positions.

**Fiscal Sponsorship Context**

Project Directors and personnel of fiscally sponsored projects are expected to comply with this Code in their Beacon-related activities. Beacon recognizes that sponsored projects maintain their own organizational cultures and may adopt additional or more specific standards. Where a sponsored project's own code of conduct exists, the more protective standard applies.

**Violations**

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action proportionate to the severity of the violation, up to and including termination of employment, termination of a contractor or volunteer relationship, removal from a board or committee position, or termination of a fiscal sponsorship arrangement. Beacon will follow fair procedures in assessing alleged violations, including providing the person concerned an opportunity to respond.

**Review**

This Code of Conduct will be reviewed annually by the Board.
