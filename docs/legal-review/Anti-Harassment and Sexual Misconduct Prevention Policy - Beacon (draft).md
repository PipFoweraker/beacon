**ANTI-HARASSMENT AND SEXUAL MISCONDUCT PREVENTION POLICY**

**Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk**

## 1. Purpose and Commitment

Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk ("Beacon") is committed to providing a work environment free from harassment, sexual misconduct, and discrimination. Beacon has a positive duty to take proactive steps to prevent unlawful conduct — not merely to respond after harm occurs.

This policy establishes Beacon's standards, complaint procedures, and preventive framework. It is designed to meet or exceed the requirements of all jurisdictions in which Beacon operates, including:

- United States: Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, applicable state and local laws (including New York, California, and Delaware requirements)
- United Kingdom: Equality Act 2010, Worker Protection (Amendment of Equality Act 2010) Act 2023, and the Employment Rights Act 2025 provisions effective October 2026
- Australia: Sex Discrimination Act 1984 as amended by the Respect@Work Act 2022

Where jurisdictions differ, the more protective standard applies.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All directors, officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, interns, and agents of Beacon
- Project Directors and personnel of fiscally sponsored projects in the context of Beacon-affiliated activities
- Third parties interacting with Beacon's people in work-related contexts (clients, donors, vendors, event attendees)

This policy applies in all work-related settings, including:

- Offices and workspaces (including home offices during remote work)
- Beacon-sponsored or Beacon-affiliated events, conferences, and meetings
- Work-related travel
- Online and electronic communications related to Beacon's work
- Social events connected to the workplace

## 3. Definitions

**Harassment** is unwanted conduct related to a protected characteristic that has the purpose or effect of violating a person's dignity, or creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment. Protected characteristics include: race, color, national origin, ethnicity, sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, pregnancy, marital status, and any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

**Sexual harassment** includes but is not limited to:

- Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature
- Conduct where submission to or rejection of such conduct is used as the basis for decisions affecting a person's employment, engagement, or participation
- Conduct that unreasonably interferes with a person's work performance or creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment
- Sexual comments, jokes, or innuendo; display of sexually explicit material; unwelcome physical contact; stalking; sexual assault

**Sex-based harassment** is harassment related to a person's sex, gender, or gender identity that is not necessarily sexual in nature. This includes hostile conduct based on sex stereotyping, pregnancy, or caregiving responsibilities.

**Hostile work environment** exists when conduct based on a protected characteristic is sufficiently severe or pervasive to alter the conditions of employment or participation and create an abusive environment.

**Victimisation** (retaliation) is subjecting a person to detriment because they have made or supported a complaint, participated in an investigation, or exercised any right under this policy.

A single incident can constitute harassment if sufficiently severe. Conduct need not be intentional to constitute harassment — the effect on the recipient is relevant regardless of intent.

## 4. Prohibited Conduct

Beacon prohibits:

1. All forms of harassment and sexual harassment as defined above
2. Sex-based harassment and creation of hostile work environments
3. Victimisation or retaliation against any person who reports, complains about, or participates in an investigation of harassment
4. Failure by any person in a supervisory or management role to take appropriate action when they become aware of potential harassment
5. Knowingly making a false complaint of harassment (distinct from a complaint made in good faith that cannot be substantiated)

## 5. Responsibilities

**The Board** is responsible for governance-level oversight of this policy, including annual review, ensuring adequate resources for implementation, and receiving reports on compliance.

**The President** (or designee) is responsible for day-to-day administration of this policy, including ensuring training is delivered, complaints are handled, and preventive measures are implemented.

**All managers and supervisors** must:
- Model respectful behavior
- Take all complaints and reports seriously
- Report any harassment they witness or learn about, whether or not a formal complaint is made
- Support persons who raise concerns
- Not engage in or tolerate retaliation

**All covered persons** must:
- Comply with this policy
- Report conduct they believe may violate this policy
- Cooperate with investigations
- Maintain confidentiality regarding complaints and investigations to the extent requested

## 6. Reporting Procedures

Beacon provides multiple channels for raising concerns. A person who experiences or witnesses conduct they believe may violate this policy may use any of the following:

**a. Direct supervisor or manager** — unless the supervisor is the subject of the complaint.

**b. The President** — for complaints involving any person other than the President.

**c. Any member of the Board of Directors** — for complaints involving the President, or where the complainant is not comfortable using other channels.

**d. The audit committee chair** — for complaints requiring formal investigation or involving senior personnel.

**e. Written submission** — complaints may be submitted in writing (email or letter) to any of the above persons.

**f. Anonymous reporting** — Beacon accepts anonymous reports. Anonymous reports will be investigated to the extent possible, though Beacon's ability to investigate and take action may be limited by the information available. No person is required to report harassment to the individual who is engaging in the harassing conduct.

Reports should be made as promptly as possible. However, there is no time limit for making a report under this policy. Late reporting does not preclude investigation or action.

## 7. Complaint Handling and Investigation

**7.1 Acknowledgment.** All complaints will be acknowledged within 3 business days of receipt.

**7.2 Interim measures.** Upon receiving a complaint, Beacon will promptly assess whether interim protective measures are necessary to protect the complainant, witnesses, or the integrity of the investigation. Interim measures may include separation of the parties, temporary changes to reporting lines or work assignments, or other steps proportionate to the circumstances. Interim measures are not disciplinary and do not imply a finding of wrongdoing.

**7.3 Investigation.** All complaints will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and as impartially as possible. Investigations will be conducted by a person with appropriate training and without a conflict of interest in the outcome.

- For complaints involving directors or officers, or complaints of a serious nature, Beacon may engage an external investigator. - Both the complainant and the respondent will have the opportunity to present their account and any supporting information.
- Investigations will be conducted with appropriate sensitivity and will seek to minimize unnecessary intrusion into the privacy of all parties.
- Beacon aims to complete investigations within 30 business days of initiation, though complex matters may require additional time. Both parties will be kept informed of progress.

**7.4 Findings and outcomes.** At the conclusion of the investigation, the investigator will make findings of fact and a determination as to whether this policy has been violated. Both the complainant and the respondent will be informed in writing of the outcome, subject to legal and privacy constraints.

**7.5 Disciplinary action.** If a violation is found, Beacon will take corrective action proportionate to the severity of the conduct. Actions may include but are not limited to: verbal or written warning, required training, change of duties or reporting relationships, suspension, termination of employment or engagement, removal from a board or committee position, or termination of a fiscal sponsorship arrangement.

**7.6 Appeals.** Either party may request a review of the outcome by submitting a written appeal to the Board (or, if the Board conducted the investigation, to an external reviewer) within 14 business days of receiving the outcome. The appeal will be considered by a person or persons who were not involved in the original investigation. Investigation records will be kept confidential and stored securely with access limited to those with a legitimate need.

## 8. Confidentiality

Beacon will maintain confidentiality to the greatest extent possible consistent with its obligation to investigate complaints and take corrective action. Information about a complaint will be shared only with those who have a legitimate need to know in order to investigate or resolve the matter, or as required by law.

All persons involved in a complaint or investigation are expected to maintain confidentiality. Breach of confidentiality may itself be grounds for disciplinary action.

Confidentiality protections do not prevent any person from:
- Reporting to law enforcement or government agencies
- Seeking legal advice
- Making disclosures protected by applicable whistleblower legislation

## 9. No Retaliation

Retaliation against any person who makes a good-faith complaint, participates in an investigation, or exercises any right under this policy is strictly prohibited and will itself be treated as a serious violation.

Retaliation includes but is not limited to: adverse employment actions, exclusion, intimidation, threats, or any other conduct that would discourage a reasonable person from exercising their rights under this policy.

Any person who believes they have been subjected to retaliation should report it immediately using any of the channels described in Section 6.

## 10. Prevention and Proactive Measures

Beacon is committed to preventing harassment, not merely responding to it. In accordance with its positive duty obligations, Beacon will:

**10.1 Risk assessment.** Beacon will conduct a harassment risk assessment within 6 months of adopting this policy and update it annually or when there is a material change to operations. The assessment will consider factors including: power imbalances, remote and isolated work situations, events involving alcohol, interactions with external parties, and the demographics and dynamics of the workforce.

**10.2 Training.**
- All covered persons will receive anti-harassment training within 30 days of onboarding and annually thereafter
- Training will be interactive and include practical examples relevant to Beacon's operations
- Managers and supervisors will receive additional training on their responsibilities, including how to receive and respond to complaints
- Training attendance will be recorded

**10.3 Communication.** This policy will be provided to all covered persons at onboarding, posted in accessible locations (including Beacon's internal documentation), and recirculated annually.

**10.4 Culture.** Beacon's leadership will actively model and promote a culture of respect, inclusion, and accountability. Feedback mechanisms will be maintained to allow people to raise concerns about workplace culture without necessarily making a formal complaint.

## 11. External Reporting Rights

Nothing in this policy prevents any person from filing a complaint with an external agency or pursuing legal remedies. Relevant agencies include:

- **United States:** Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC); applicable state and local human rights agencies
- **United Kingdom:** Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC); employment tribunal system
- **Australia:** Australian Human Rights Commission (AHRC); Fair Work Commission; state and territory anti-discrimination bodies

Persons may pursue external complaints concurrently with or instead of Beacon's internal procedures. Beacon encourages but does not require internal reporting before external action.

## 12. Monitoring and Review

The Board will review this policy annually. Reviews will consider:

- Number and nature of complaints received
- Outcomes of investigations
- Effectiveness of training and preventive measures
- Feedback from covered persons (including, where appropriate, anonymous surveys)
- Changes in applicable law or funder requirements

The Board will receive a summary compliance report at least annually, in a form that protects the confidentiality of individuals.

## 13. Interaction with Other Policies

This policy operates alongside Beacon's:
- Code of Conduct
- Conflict of Interest Policy
- Whistleblower Policy
- Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy

Where conduct may violate multiple policies, the most protective provisions apply. Reports made under this policy that also engage the Whistleblower Policy will receive the protections of both.
