# Board Member Responsibilities

**Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk**

This document summarizes what is expected of you as a director of Beacon. It is not a legal document — the governing legal instruments are the Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, and adopted policies. This is a practical overview for onboarding conversations.

## Legal Duties

As a director of a Delaware nonprofit corporation, you have three fiduciary duties:

**Duty of Care.** Pay reasonable attention to Beacon's affairs. Read materials before meetings. Ask questions when something is unclear. You don't need to be an expert in everything, but you need to exercise the care that an ordinarily prudent person would in a similar position.

**Duty of Loyalty.** Put Beacon's interests ahead of your own. Disclose any conflicts of interest. Recuse yourself from decisions where you have a personal financial interest. Complete the annual Conflict of Interest disclosure form.

**Duty of Obedience.** Ensure Beacon operates within its stated charitable mission and complies with applicable law. Don't approve actions that would jeopardize Beacon's 501(c)(3) status.

## What Beacon Needs From You

**Core obligations (all directors):**

- Attend board meetings as required (initially monthly during launch phase, expected to move to bimonthly or quarterly once operations stabilize)
- Review materials circulated before meetings
- Vote yes / no / abstain on proposed projects for fiscal sponsorship
- Vote on other matters brought before the Board (budgets, policies, officer appointments, etc.)
- Complete an annual Conflict of Interest disclosure form
- Disclose any conflicts as they arise, before relevant decisions
- Respond to written consent requests in a timely manner (for decisions taken without a meeting, all directors must consent)

**If you are the Secretary:**

- Ensure minutes are taken at all Board meetings (Beacon's admin assistant will draft minutes; the Secretary reviews and certifies them)
- Ensure proper notice is given for meetings
- Maintain the corporate record book

**If you are the Treasurer:**

- Oversee that adequate financial records are maintained (Beacon staff and the outsourced accountant will prepare financial statements and reports; the Treasurer reviews them for the Board)
- Ensure funds are properly deposited and disbursed per Board authorization
- Present financial reports to the Board

In practice, Beacon staff will do the operational work for both roles. The Secretary and Treasurer provide oversight, review, and sign-off — not day-to-day execution.

## What Beacon Does NOT Expect

- Day-to-day management (that's the President/CEO's job)
- Fundraising obligations (welcome but not required)
- Financial contributions (welcome but not required)
- Expertise in nonprofit law or accounting (though it helps)

## Liability Protection

Beacon's bylaws provide broad indemnification for directors acting in good faith. Beacon intends to obtain D&O insurance to back this up. Volunteer directors of nonprofits also receive some protection under the federal Volunteer Protection Act (42 U.S.C. section 14501 et seq.) for actions taken in good faith within the scope of their responsibilities.

## Time Commitment (Estimate)

- Board meetings: 1-2 hours per meeting, monthly initially
- Pre-meeting review: 30-60 minutes per meeting
- Occasional email votes or consent actions between meetings
- Annual conflict of interest disclosure: 15 minutes

Estimated total: 3-5 hours/month during launch phase, decreasing as operations stabilize.
