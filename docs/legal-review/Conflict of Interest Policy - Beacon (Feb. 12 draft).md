**CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY**

The purpose of this Conflict of Interest Policy (the "Policy") is to
protect the interests of Beacon Institute for Global Catastrophic Risk
("Beacon") when it is contemplating entering into a transaction or
arrangement that might benefit the private interest of a director,
officer, employee, or identified contractors of Beacon (a "covered
person") or might result in a possible excess benefit transaction. This
Policy is intended to supplement but not replace any applicable state
and federal laws governing conflicts of interest applicable to nonprofit
and charitable organizations.

**Article I. Definitions**

*Compensation.* Compensation includes direct and indirect remuneration
as well as gifts or favors that are not insubstantial.

C*onflict of Interest.* A conflict of interest exists when a covered
person's financial or other interests may impair, or reasonably appear
to impair, the individual's ability to act in the best interests of
Beacon.

*Family*. Family includes one's spouse, domestic partner, ancestors,
descendants, siblings, and their respective spouses or domestic
partners, and any other person with whom one has a similarly close
personal relationship.

*Financial Interest*. A person has a financial interest if the person
has, directly or indirectly, through business, investment, or family:

a\. An actual or planned ownership or investment interest in any entity
with which Beacon has a transaction or arrangement,

b\. An actual or planned compensation arrangement with any entity or
individual with which Beacon has a transaction or arrangement, or

c\. An actual or planned ownership or investment interest in, or
compensation arrangement with, any entity or individual with which
Beacon is negotiating a transaction or arrangement.

A financial interest is not necessarily a conflict of interest. Under
Article II, a person who has a financial interest may have a conflict of
interest only if the appropriate decisionmaker decides that a conflict
of interest exists.

*Interested Person*. Any covered person who has a financial interest is
an interested person. A person who has any other bias that may impair
their ability to act in Beacon's best interests is also an interested
person. A person who may reasonably appear to have such a financial
interest or bias is also an interested person.

**Article II. Procedures**

1\. Duty to Disclose. Any interested person must disclose the existence
of the actual or possible conflict of interest and all material facts
relating to the relevant transaction or arrangement. Directors and
officers must make this disclosure to Beacon's board of directors (the
"Board") or a delegated committee. Employees and contractors must make
this disclosure to their manager. Any person aware of an actual or
possible conflict of interest must remind the interested person to make
disclosure or notify the interested person's manager or the Board.

2\. Recusal; Effect of Recusal. In all situations calling for
disclosure, the interested person must not participate in any relevant
decision other than by providing information requested by the
decisionmaker, unless and until the decisionmaker determines that there
is not a conflict.

If the actual or possible conflict of interest does not involve a
director or officer, and was identified early such that the relevant
person has been recused from all participation, the transaction or
arrangement may be approved by their manager or other authorized
persons. If the actual or possible conflict of interest does not involve
a director or officer and the relevant person's manager determines that
there is no conflict of interest, the transaction or arrangement may
proceed in its ordinary course, provided that the manager must provide
notice and a summary to the Board. Since a reasonable appearance can
constitute a conflict of interest, decisionmakers should err on the side
of caution and either require the recusal of the individual or the
procedures below in the event of any doubt.

3\. Board and Committee Review of Conflicts of Interest

If the actual or possible conflict of interest involves a director or
officer, or an interested person was involved in the decisionmaking for
the transaction or arrangement prior to recusal, the Board or delegated
committee will take the following steps prior to approval of the
relevant transaction or arrangement:

a\. The interested person may make a presentation at the Board or
committee meeting. After the presentation and outside the presence of
the interested person, the Board or committee will determine whether
there is a conflict of interest. Since a reasonable appearance can
constitute a conflict of interest, decisionmakers should err on the side
of caution and require the procedures below in the event of any doubt.

b\. The chair of the Board or committee shall, if appropriate, appoint a
disinterested person or committee to investigate alternatives to the
relevant transaction or arrangement.

c\. After exercising due diligence, the Board or committee shall
determine whether Beacon can obtain, with reasonable efforts, a more
advantageous transaction or arrangement from a person or entity that
would not give rise to a conflict of interest.

d\. If a more advantageous transaction or arrangement isn't reasonably
possible under circumstances not producing a conflict of interest, the
Board or committee shall determine by a majority vote of the
disinterested directors or committee members whether the transaction or
arrangement is in Beacon's best interests and whether it is fair and
reasonable. In conformity with the above determination, it shall make
its decision as to whether to enter into the transaction or arrangement.

In no event will the Board or committee approve an excess benefit
transaction as defined in Section 4958 of the Internal Revenue Code.

4\. Violations of the Conflict of Interest Policy

If the Board or committee has reasonable cause to believe anyone has
failed to disclose actual or possible conflicts of interest, it shall
inform the person of the basis for such belief and afford the person an
opportunity to explain the alleged failure to disclose. If, after
hearing the person's response and after making further investigation as
warranted by the circumstances, the Board or committee determines the
person has failed to disclose an actual or possible conflict of
interest, it shall take appropriate disciplinary and corrective action.

**Article III. Records of Proceedings**

The minutes of the Board and all committees with board delegated powers
shall contain:

a\. The names of the persons who disclosed or otherwise were found to
have an actual or possible conflict of interest, the nature of the
conflict, any action taken to determine whether a conflict of interest
was present, and the Board's or committee's decision as to whether a
conflict of interest in fact existed.

b\. The names of the persons who were present for discussions and votes
relating to the transaction or arrangement, the content of the
discussion, including any alternatives to the relevant transaction or
arrangement, and a record of any votes taken in connection with the
proceedings.

**Article IV. Compensation**

A voting member of the Board who receives compensation, directly or
indirectly, from Beacon for services is precluded from voting on matters
pertaining to that member's compensation. A voting member of any
committee whose jurisdiction includes compensation matters and who
receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from Beacon for services
is precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that member's
compensation.

No member of the Board or any committee is prohibited from providing
information to any committee regarding compensation.

Covered persons may not accept any payments, gifts, loans, or other
favors from anyone who is doing, or wishes to do, business with Beacon,
except for items of nominal value (under \$50 per year per source) or as
approved by the Board or designee.

**Article V. Annual Statements and Disclosures**

1\. Each covered person shall annually sign a statement which affirms
such person:

a\. Has received a copy of the Policy,

b\. Has read and understands the Policy,

c\. Has agreed to comply with the Policy, and

d\. Understands that Beacon is a public charity and that in order to
maintain its federal tax exemption it must be operated exclusively for
its tax-exempt purposes.

2\. Upon election, hiring, or appointment, and annually thereafter,
covered persons must complete an annual disclosure questionnaire in the
form provided by Beacon and disclose all affiliations or other matters
that could give rise to a conflict of interest. Covered persons have a
continuing responsibility to review their business and personal
interests and relationships for actual or possible conflicts of
interest, and should update this disclosure mid-year as appropriate.

**Article VI. Periodic Review**

To ensure Beacon operates in a manner consistent with charitable
purposes, and does not engage in activities that could jeopardize its
tax-exempt status, periodic reviews shall be conducted. The periodic
reviews shall, at a minimum, include the following subjects:

a\. Whether compensation arrangements and benefits are reasonable, based
on competent survey information, and are the result of arm's length
bargaining.

b\. Whether partnerships, joint ventures, and arrangements with
management organizations conform to Beacon's written policies, are
properly recorded, reflect reasonable investment or payments for goods
and services, further charitable purposes, and do not result in
inurement, impermissible private benefit, or in an excess benefit
transaction.

When conducting the periodic reviews, Beacon may, but need not, use
outside advisors. If outside experts are used, their use shall not
relieve the Board of its responsibility for ensuring periodic reviews
are conducted.
