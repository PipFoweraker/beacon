## Form 1023 vs 1023-EZ Decision

### 1023-EZ Eligibility Criteria (simplified form)
- Projected gross receipts ≤ $50,000/year for each of next 3 years
- Total assets ≤ $250,000
- Not a successor to a for-profit entity
- Not a foreign organization

### Beacon's Situation
From budget projections:
- Year 1 projected operating income: ~$662k
- Year 1 projected total income (incl pass-through): ~$2.2M
- **Conclusion:** Full Form 1023 required (not eligible for 1023-EZ)

## Form 1023 Required Components

### Part I: Identification of Applicant
- [ ] Legal name (from Articles of Incorporation)
- [ ] EIN (obtain after incorporation)
- [ ] Mailing address (virtual mailbox once established)
- [ ] Contact person information
- [ ] Month accounting period ends (likely December)
- [ ] Date incorporated

### Part II: Organizational Structure
- [ ] Articles of Incorporation (certified copy)
- [ ] Bylaws (executed copy)
- [ ] Documentation showing adoption of organizing documents

### Part III: Required Provisions in Organizing Documents
Articles must contain:
- [ ] Exclusive charitable purpose clause (501(c)(3) language)
- [ ] Dissolution clause (assets to another 501(c)(3))
- [ ] No private inurement clause

### Part IV: Narrative Description of Activities
**This is the heart of the application - must be detailed and compelling**

Key elements to address:
- [ ] Mission statement and charitable purpose
- [ ] Specific activities and programs (fiscal sponsorship model)
- [ ] Who benefits (GCR/AI safety researchers and projects)
- [ ] How activities further exempt purposes
- [ ] Geographic scope (US-based, international projects)
- [ ] Relationship with sponsored projects
- [ ] Fee structure (10% admin fee on restricted funds)

Draft narrative sections needed:
1. Introduction and mission
2. Problem statement (admin friction for GCR research)
3. Fiscal sponsorship model explanation
4. Project intake and diligence process
5. Governance and controls
6. Public benefit analysis

### Part V: Compensation and Financial Arrangements
- [ ] Compensation of officers, directors, trustees
- [ ] Compensation of highest-paid employees
- [ ] Independent contractor arrangements
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy
- [ ] Compensation review process

Key disclosures:
- Pip Foweraker: Interim CEO, $120k/year (0.8 FTE)
- Certes secondment arrangement (related party - needs careful disclosure)

### Part VI: Financial Data
- [ ] Statement of Revenue and Expenses (actual + projected)
- [ ] Balance Sheet
- [ ] 3-year financial projections

From budget model:

|------|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| 2026 | $662k | $603k | ~$1.6M |
| 2027 | TBD | TBD | TBD |

### Part VII: Foundation Classification
Beacon will likely seek classification as:
- **509(a)(1)** - publicly supported organization, OR
- **509(a)(2)** - organization receiving income from exempt activities

Key test: Public support test (>33.33% from public sources)
- Seed funding from AISTOF counts as public support
- Admin fees from fiscal sponsorship count
- Need to demonstrate broad donor base over time

### Part VIII: Effective Date
- Can be retroactive to incorporation if filed within 27 months
- Beacon can operate as 501(c)(3) pending determination
